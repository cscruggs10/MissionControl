## Thread #1: The Augusta Rule ($14K Tax-Free Loophole)

### Strategic Goal
**Primary:** Viral awareness (this has highest viral potential)  
**Secondary:** Position Ajax as source of "insider" tax knowledge  
**Lead Gen:** Drive to discovery call booking

### Compliance Guardrails ⚖️

**MUST INCLUDE:**
- "Legitimate business purpose required" (tweet 5)
- "Consult tax advisor for your situation" (final tweet)
- Fair market rent language (no inflated rates)
- Documentation emphasis

**AVOID:**
- "Guaranteed" tax savings claims
- "IRS doesn't care" or anti-IRS sentiment
- Suggesting fake business meetings
- Absolute statements ("You will save...")

**Safe Language:**
- "If properly structured..."
- "When legitimate business purpose exists..."
- "Potential savings for qualifying situations..."
- "Work with qualified advisor to implement..."

### Message Architecture

**Hook (Tweet 1):**  
Lead with curiosity + specific dollar amount. Frame as "overlooked" not "secret loophole."

Example angle: "Most business owners miss this: rent your home to your business 14 days/year, keep $14K+ tax-free. Here's what your CPA probably didn't tell you:"

**Education Middle (Tweets 2-7):**
- Tweet 2: What is Augusta Rule (Section 280A origin story)
- Tweet 3: Mechanics (how it works step-by-step)
- Tweet 4: Real example with numbers ($1,500/day x 14 days = $21K tax-free)
- Tweet 5: IRS requirements (MUST emphasize legitimate purpose)
- Tweet 6: Common mistakes (inflated rates, no documentation, fake meetings)
- Tweet 7: Who qualifies (business entity types)

**CTA Close (Tweet 8):**  
Soft ask → discovery call to explore tax strategy. Include booking link.

### Tactical Notes for Blaster

- Keep tweets 240-280 characters (room for engagement)
- Use specific numbers, not ranges when possible
- Thread should feel like "insider knowledge share" not ad
- Include 1-2 questions to encourage replies (Tweet 3 or 6)
- Avoid jargon dumps — translate tax code to human language

**Example of good vs. bad translation:**
- ❌ Bad: "IRC Section 280A(g) provides rental income exclusion for dwelling units"
- ✅ Good: "The IRS allows you to rent your home 14 days/year without reporting income — it's called the Augusta Rule"

### Success Metrics
- 500+ likes (viral threshold)
- 50+ retweets
- 20+ comments/questions
- 5+ booking link clicks

## Thread #2: Entity Structure Mistake ($52K Cost)

### Strategic Goal
**Primary:** Establish authority through case study storytelling  
**Secondary:** Challenge conventional wisdom (S-Corp isn't always optimal)  
**Lead Gen:** Position entity optimization as service offering

### Compliance Guardrails ⚖️

**MUST INCLUDE:**
- "Case study for educational purposes" framing (tweet 2)
- "Individual results vary" disclaimer (tweet 10)
- "Not one-size-fits-all advice" (tweet 8)
- "Consult tax and legal advisors" (final tweet)

**AVOID:**
- Guaranteeing specific dollar savings
- Suggesting S-Corp is "bad" universally
- Oversimplifying complex QBI rules
- Absolute timing recommendations ("Always wait until...")

**Safe Language:**
- "In this client's situation..."
- "May cost six figures depending on..."
- "Break-even analysis required for each business..."
- "Proper timing depends on multiple factors..."

### Message Architecture

**Hook (Tweet 1):**  
Lead with shocking dollar loss. Create pattern interrupt (S-Corp conventional wisdom challenged).

Example angle: "Choosing S-Corp election at the wrong time cost my client $52,379 in one year. Here's the entity timing trap that bleeds six figures:"

**Story Arc (Tweets 2-9):**
- Tweet 2: Meet the client (relatable: $180K revenue, growing fast)
- Tweet 3: The decision (CPA said "elect S-Corp now")
- Tweet 4: What they didn't know (QBI deduction phase-out)
- Tweet 5: The math (show the $52K calculation clearly)
- Tweet 6: When S-Corp DOES make sense (balance, not bash)
- Tweet 7: Break-even framework (give actionable decision tool)
- Tweet 8: The fix (entity optimization + captive insurance bridge)
- Tweet 9: Key takeaway (proper timing = strategy not guessing)

**CTA Close (Tweet 10):**  
Offer entity structure analysis. Booking link.

### Tactical Notes for Blaster

- This is STORY thread, not list thread — maintain narrative flow
- Make the client relatable (mid-sized business owner, not Fortune 500)
- Show the math visually if possible (or very clearly)
- Balance cautionary tale with solution (not just fear)
- Include 1 contrarian insight that feels fresh (S-Corp timing nuance)

**Tone calibration:**
- Don't bash CPAs generally (we want their referrals)
- Frame as "reactive tax prep vs. proactive tax strategy"
- Position Ajax as the "strategic layer" above compliance

### Strategic Goal
**Primary:** Educational value bomb (bookmark/save-worthy)  
**Secondary:** Showcase breadth of Ajax expertise  
**Lead Gen:** Comprehensive tax strategy audit offer

### Compliance Guardrails ⚖️

**MUST INCLUDE:**
- "If qualified" or "when applicable" for EACH deduction
- Documentation requirements emphasized
- "Work with qualified advisor" (final tweet)
- "These are examples, not promises" framing

**AVOID:**
- "Everyone qualifies for these"
- Suggesting deductions without conditions
- Downplaying audit risk
- Guaranteed dollar amounts

**Safe Language:**
- "If your business qualifies..."
- "When properly documented..."
- "Up to [amount] depending on situation..."
- "Requires compliance with IRS requirements..."

- **Name + Dollar Potential**
- **What it is (brief)**
- **Who qualifies (conditional)**
- **One actionable insight**

Example format:
"3/ Home Office Depreciation (not just square footage)

Beyond the simple square footage deduction, you can depreciate the actual structure over 39 years. On a $400K home with 15% business use, that's an extra $1,538/year most miss.

Requirement: Exclusive business use of space."

**Deduction List Priority Order:**
1. Captive insurance premiums ($1.2M potential — our core offering)

3. Home office depreciation (relatable, accessible)
4. Vehicle actual expense (common mistake to fix)
5. Augusta Rule (callback to Thread #1)
6. Family payroll (engaging, controversy potential)
7. Self-directed retirement + life insurance (advanced)

**Educational Close (Tweets 10-12):**
- Tweet 10: How to implement (process overview)
- Tweet 11: Red flags to avoid (audit protection angle)
- Tweet 12: When to get help (our CTA)

**CTA Close (Tweet 13):**  
Comprehensive tax strategy audit offer. Booking link.

### Tactical Notes for Blaster

- List format = easy to consume, high save/bookmark rate
- Keep each deduction tweet to 240-260 characters (consistency)
- Use specific dollar examples (not ranges when possible)
- Vary the conditional language ("If qualified" / "When applicable" / "Depending on situation") — avoid repetitive
- Position captive insurance naturally (our core service) but don't oversell in thread

**Engagement optimization:**
- Number each tweet (1/, 2/, 3/... format)
- Include mini-cliffhanger in tweets 1-2 (list format creates curiosity)
- Ask engagement question around tweet 6-7 ("Which of these surprised you most?")

**Compliance double-check:**
- Each deduction must have a qualifier
- No absolute promises
