## Market Segmentation Strategy

### Why Geographic Focus Matters

**Why not national from day 1?**
- Impossible to do well (too many targets)
- Higher rejection rate with no local credibility
- Harder to follow up (timezone management)
- Harder to get Loom views (less personal connection)
- Better to own a market, then expand

**Better approach:**
- Pick 1-3 geographic markets for first wave
- Build credibility + wins in those markets
- Use those wins as social proof to expand
- Then go national with proven playbook

---

## Market Selection Criteria

**Good Markets Have:**

1. **High concentration of target audience**
   - Business owner population (e.g., Memphis has strong auto/wholesale culture)
   - Middle-market adviso density (firms with $50M-$500M AUM)
   - CPA/tax professional population (look for major business hubs)

2. **Regional economic drivers**
   - Industries with high tax burden (real estate, professional services, business owners)
   - Entrepreneurial ecosystem (startup hubs, manufacturing regions)
   - Business owner wealth concentration (not just employees)

3. **Accessibility for personal touch**
   - Can do in-person follow-ups if needed
   - Time zone manageable
   - Regional reputation/network

4. **Outreach response patterns**
   - Mid-market firms more likely to respond than mega-firms or solopreneurs
   - Tax-focused practices more likely than investment-only advisors
   - Owner-operated firms more likely than employee-run

## Identifying Target CPAs

### Profile: "The Right CPA"

**Do target:**
- ✅ CPAs with own practices (not employees at mega-firms)
- ✅ Tax specialists (not just bookkeepers)
- ✅ Serve business owners + high-net-worth clients
- ✅ Firms with 2-15 professionals (mid-size)
- ✅ Show interest in strategy/differentiation
- ✅ Active on LinkedIn (posted in last 30 days)

**Don't target:**
- ❌ Mega-firms (Big 4: Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PWC) — won't respond to outreach
- ❌ Solo practitioners with no staff (can't execute)
- ❌ Purely compliance-focused firms (not interested in strategy)
- ❌ Mega advisory firms (500+ employees) — bureaucratic
- ❌ Firms with no online presence (unlikely to engage)

**Red flags:**
- ❌ No LinkedIn profile or inactive
- ❌ Website outdated (last updated 3+ years ago)
- ❌ All their content is tax compliance articles
- ❌ They exclusively serve one narrow industry
- ❌ Comments don't show strategic thinking

## Finding & Researching Targets

### Method 1: Apollo.io (Primary Tool)

**Setup:**
- Create Apollo account (free tier available, $99/mo for full access)
- Install Chrome extension for bulk lookup

**Search filters:**
- Title: "CPA" OR "Accountant" OR "Tax Manager"
- Company size: 2-50 employees
- Location: [Your target market]
- Industry: Tax/Accounting Services OR Professional Services
- Lead quality: "Verified emails" (if available)

**Output:**
- Export CSV with name, email, company, LinkedIn, phone
- ~200-500 results per market (you'll qualify down to ~50)

### Method 2: LinkedIn Search (Supplemental)

**Search string:**
```
(CPA OR "certified public accountant" OR "tax accountant" OR "tax strategist") 
AND "business owner" 
AND (CEO OR founder OR partner) 
AND (Memphis OR Austin OR Denver) 
AND recent activity
```

**Qualification:**
- [ ] Posted or commented in last 30 days
- [ ] Content shows strategic thinking (not just tax compliance)
- [ ] Serve business owners (evident from content/profile)
- [ ] Firm size 2-20 people (based on profile description)

### Method 3: Google Search (High-Intent)

**Search patterns:**
- "[City] tax consultant business owners"
- "[City] CPA tax strategy firms"
- "[City] fractional CFO business advisors"
- "[City] strategic tax planning"

**Qualification:**
- [ ] Website looks professional (updated, not 2005 design)
- [ ] Blog/content shows strategic thinking
- [ ] Team page has 3-15 people
- [ ] Contact info easily available
- [ ] Services include "tax strategy" or "planning," not just prep

### Method 4: Chamber of Commerce / Referrals

**Approach:**
- Search local chamber sites (they list members)
- Ask people you know for referrals ("Who's the best tax advisor for business owners in [city]?")
- Network through professional organizations

**Advantage:** Warm intro context helps conversion

## Building Your Target List

### Step 1: Research & Qualification

**For each target:**
1. Find email + LinkedIn profile
2. Review their website + last 3 LinkedIn posts
3. Verify they fit the profile (business owner focus, strategic thinking)
4. Rate them 1-5 (5 = perfect fit, 1 = probably not)
5. Note personalization angle (what specific thing makes them good?)

**Example research doc:**

| Name | Company | Title | Email | LinkedIn | Fit (1-5) | Angle | Source |
|------|---------|-------|-------|----------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Sarah Chen | Chen CPA | Partner | sarah@chen-cpa.com | /in/sarahchen | 5 | Recent post on business valuation for exits | Apollo |
| Mike Johnson | Johnson & Co | Managing Dir | mike@johnsonco.com | /in/mikejohnson | 4 | Specializes in real estate tax planning | LinkedIn |
| Elizabeth Park | Park Advisors | Principal | liz@parkadvisors.com | /in/lizpark | 3 | Good firm size, but content is mostly compliance | Google |

### Step 2: Prioritize

**Scoring (pick top 50 by score):**

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Firm size 2-20 people | +5 |
| Recent LinkedIn activity (last 30d) | +5 |
| Posting about strategy/planning | +5 |
| Serves business owners explicitly | +5 |
| Unique angle for personalization | +3 |
| Warm intro available | +3 |
| Website updated within 12 months | +2 |

**Score 20+:** Top priority (reach out first)
**Score 15-19:** Secondary tier (reach out week 2)
**Score 10-14:** Tertiary tier (reach out week 3+)
**Score <10:** Don't reach out (probably won't respond)

- Name, title, company

## Personalization Research Angle

**What to look for (for Loom/email angle):**

1. **Their recent LinkedIn posts:**
   - Article about tax planning? Reference it
   - Post about business valuation? Great angle
   - Post about retirement planning? Use that
   - Posted about their firm's growth? Reference that

2. **Their website/services page:**
   - Do they mention business owners specifically? Lead with that
   - Do they mention "strategy" vs. just "preparation"? Lead with strategy
   - Do they mention specific industries? Reference those industries

3. **Their firm's focus:**
   - Real estate heavy? Angle toward real estate investor clients
   - Professional services? Angle toward doctors/lawyers/dentists
   - Manufacturing? Angle toward family business owners

**Example angles:**

"Hi Sarah, I noticed you just posted about business exit planning — that's exactly the strategy we solve for business owners you're already working with."

"Hi Mike, your focus on real estate investor tax strategy is impressive. Real estate investors are our sweet spot, and there's a strategy most accountants don't know about..."

"Hi Elizabeth, I saw your firm specializes in dental practices. Those owners are exactly who we help with a unique tax strategy..."
