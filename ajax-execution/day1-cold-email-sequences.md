*Created: Feb 12, 2026 — 12:05 PM UTC*
*Framework: 3-part sequence for CPA/Wealth Advisor outreach*

---

## Email 1: THE OPENER (Pattern Interrupt)

**Subject:** Something specific about THEM (personalized, not generic)

**Goal:** Get them to read + build curiosity

**Framework:**

```
Hi [Name],

I noticed [something specific about their firm/LinkedIn/niche] — impressed by how you're [specific thing they do well].

I work with forward-thinking CPAs and wealth advisors who want to add differentiation without the infrastructure headache.

Specifically: we handle the complex stuff so you can offer tax-advantaged captive insurance strategies to your business owner clients — positioning yourself as the strategic advisor, not the commodity tax guy.

Quick question: are your business owner clients asking you "how can I reduce taxes?" more than you're asking them?

If so, might be worth a quick 15-min chat about how this could look.

Worth a conversation?

[Your name]
[Your title]
[Phone]
```

**Personalization variables:**
- [something specific] — reference their website, a LinkedIn post, a blog article, their firm size, a niche they serve
- [specific thing they do well] — could be thought leadership, niching down, client retention, reputation

**Length:** 100-120 words (short enough to read in 30 seconds)

**CTA:** Soft, permission-based (not "let's hop on a call" immediately)

## Email 2: THE VALUE LAYER (Follow-up at +3 days)

**Subject Line:** Re: [original subject] or new angle

**Goal:** If they didn't respond, provide value that demonstrates competence

**Framework (Pick ONE angle based on their profile):**

### Angle A: For Tax-Focused CPAs
```
Hi [Name],

Quick add to my last note — your clients' tax frustrations are exactly what we solve.

Here's what we typically see:
- Business owners paying $50K-$500K+ in taxes they didn't know they could reduce
- CPAs recommending traditional strategies (S-corp elections, depreciation) that miss bigger opportunities
- Advisors stuck competing on price, not strategy

Captive insurance (when properly structured) lets business owners:
- Create legitimate insurance deductions ($100K-$500K+)
- Build tax-deferred wealth inside their business
- Reduce tax burden while improving asset protection

It's not a loophole. It's a strategy that's been around 30+ years. Most CPAs simply haven't built the infrastructure.

We handle the infrastructure. You handle the client relationship. You look like a genius.

Would a 15-min call to explore whether this fits your practice be useful?

[Your name]
```

### Angle B: For Advisors/RIAs (Differentiation Focus)
```
Hi [Name],

Follow-up thought on my last email:

Your business owner clients are sitting on a major opportunity they don't know exists. And your competitors aren't talking about it either.

Most advisors compete on:
- Better investment performance (commoditized)
- Lower fees (race to bottom)
- "Personalized service" (everyone says this)

What if you competed on tax strategy instead?

We help advisors offer sophisticated tax-advantaged captive insurance strategies to business owner clients. Suddenly, you're not the guy competing on fee and returns — you're the strategic partner helping them build wealth AND reduce taxes.

It differentiates you. It deepens client relationships. It's genuinely valuable.

Would a brief conversation about what this looks like for your practice be worth your time?

[Your name]
```

### Angle C: For Larger Firms (Revenue Add-on)
```
Hi [Name],

Quick follow-up — I wanted to highlight the revenue opportunity here.

We work with advisory firms who add captive insurance consulting to their service menu. Here's the economics:

- Setup fees: $15K-$50K per client
- Ongoing management: 10-20% of annual premium (recurring)
- Risk to you: Zero (we handle the technical infrastructure)

For a firm with $100M+ in AUM, even 5-10 captive clients = $75K-$250K in setup revenue + recurring management fees.

And it deepens your relationship with business owner clients (your most profitable segment).

Worth 15 minutes to see if this makes sense for your practice?

[Your name]
```

**Length:** 150-200 words (longer = more value demonstration)

**Tone:** Specific value prop, not generic sales pitch

**CTA:** Still soft (not pushy)

## Email 3: THE SOCIAL PROOF (Follow-up at +7 days)

**Subject:** One more thing... (new angle, not defensive)

**Goal:** If still no response, provide proof/credibility + final soft ask

**Framework:**

```
Hi [Name],

One final thought, then I'll stop bugging you. :)

We've worked with [NUMBER] CPAs and advisors in [REGION/INDUSTRY] who've added captive strategies to their practice. Here's what they've told us:

**Why they wanted to do it:**
- Needed differentiation in commoditized market
- Wanted to serve business owner clients better
- Looking for additional revenue streams

**Why it worked:**
- Clients actually want this (proven demand)
- We handle the infrastructure (they own the relationship)
- It deepens trust with business owner clients
- Recurring revenue + setup fees

**What they wish they'd known:**
- Should have started sooner (first client validates the model)
- Takes 2-3 months to build pipeline, but then compounds

If this resonates at all, I'd genuinely love to show you what we've built and how it could work for your practice.

No pressure — if it's not a fit, totally understand.

If you're curious though, let's hop on a quick 15-min call this week?

[Your name]
[Phone]
[Calendar link if available]
```

**Length:** 180-220 words

**Key elements:**
- Named social proof (number of advisors, but NOT names without permission)
- Specific benefits they've seen
- Addressing common objections ("wish they'd known sooner")
- Final soft CTA with reduced friction (calendly link, specific time window)

## Sequencing Rules

**Standard Multi-Touch Sequence:**

| Touch | Medium | Days Out | Purpose |
|-------|--------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Loom video | Day 0 | Personalized, pattern interrupt |
| 2 | Email (Opener) | Day 1 | Follow-up, reference Loom |
| 3 | Email (Value) | Day 4 | Demonstrate competence, provide value |
| 4 | Handwritten letter | Day 7 | Pattern interrupt, high-touch |
| 5 | Phone call | Day 8-10 | Direct conversation, soft ask |
| 6 | Email (Social Proof) | Day 14 | Final touch, prove it works |
| 7 | Loom follow-up | Day 21 | Different angle, fresh hook |

**If they respond at ANY point:**
- Mark as "warm" in CRM
- Prioritize immediate follow-up
- Schedule discovery call within 24h
- Don't send remaining sequence emails (move to calendar)

## Subject Line Templates

**Pattern Interrupt (Opener):**
- "Quick question about [their niche/firm]"
- "[Company name] + tax strategy question"
- "Thought of you because..."
- "Curious about something..."

**Value Angle (Follow-up):**
- "Re: [original subject] — one more thing"
- "Add-on to my last email"
- "Quick insight about business owner tax planning"
- "Idea for [their firm name]"

**Social Proof (Final Touch):**
- "One more thing..."
- "Quick follow-up (this one's different)"
- "Thought you'd find this useful"

**General Rules:**
- No ALL CAPS
- No exclamation marks (unprofessional)
- No question marks ONLY (adds curiosity — "Quick question about [X]" > "Do you want to know about [X]?")
- Personalization always > template

---

## Personalization Checklist

Before sending ANY email, verify:

- [ ] Recipient's correct name + title
- [ ] Company name spelled correctly
- [ ] Reference something specific to THEM (not just their industry)
- [ ] Subject line is personalized, not template
- [ ] Value prop is tailored to their profile (tax-focused, differentiation-focused, revenue-focused)
- [ ] Calendar link or phone number is included (reduce friction to respond)
- [ ] No obvious copy-paste errors or placeholder brackets

## Metrics to Track

**Per sequence:**
- Open rate (goal: 40%+)
- Reply rate (goal: 3-8%)
- Meeting booked rate (goal: 0.5-2% of sends)
- Time-to-reply (measure engagement)

**By touch:**
- Loom: view rate, reply rate, booking rate
- Email 1 (Opener): open rate, reply rate
- Email 2 (Value): open rate, reply rate (often best-performing)
- Email 3 (Social Proof): open rate, reply rate (final ask)
- Handwritten letter: receipt rate (hardest to track), sentiment shift in calls
- Phone call: connect rate, meeting booked rate

**Winning metrics:**
- Best subject lines for re-testing
- Best value angles (which resonates most?)
- Best time of day/week to send (test Tues-Thu 10-11am typically wins)

---

## Notes

- These are templates. Personalization beats perfection — slightly awkward but authentic beats polished but generic.
- Test subject lines with small batches before scaling
- Track everything in CRM — what works becomes your system
- If someone responds, even with "not interested" — they're warm for future sequencing
- The goal is a discovery call, not a sale. Don't oversell in the email.
